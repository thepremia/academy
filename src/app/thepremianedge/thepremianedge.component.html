The
Premia Edge

The integrated curriculum at Premia comprises & aligns with all the learning outcomes in CBSE, IGCSE & AERO standards. The curriculum includes reading, writing, listening, speaking, social studies, math, science, health, physical education, music, and visual arts, experiential learning, public speaking, emphasis on projects, etc. We at Premia will utilize different approaches of integration best suited for the students:

Multi-Disciplinary Integration

To focus on reading, writing, and communication in all its forms, in language arts. This approach focuses on integration within one subject area, teaching the content with deeper understanding.

Inter-disciplinary integration

Focuses on project-based learning in more than one subject area. Interdisciplinary integration assembles the teaching of several subjects around common issues, or problems, highlighting thematic concepts in which a common theme is studied.

Trans-disciplinary integration

Involves understanding the student’s interests, and concerns while encouraging them to ask questions to inquire about the subject matter from a variety of perspectives. Interdisciplinary integration incorporates information, involving students’ interests and questions

Key Areas of Learning

Communication and Language

Communication and language development is about different ways a child understands and communicates, using words, gestures or facial expressions. Being an indicator of fine motor skill development and a reflection of cognitive development, reading is one of the best ways to encourage communication and language development.

Personal, Social and Emotional Development

It’s essential for children to feel comfortable, emotionally and physically to learn effectively. Helping children to learn how to regulate and manage their feelings is, therefore, a vital stepping stone for success in learning and life.

Physical Development

Children meet a myriad of physical development milestones in the first few years of life, from walking to drawing. Physical development focuses on increasing the skill and performance of the body. The development of children’s physical skills should be developed holistically across all areas of learning.
